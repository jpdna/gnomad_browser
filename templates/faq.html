Frequently Asked Questions

How should I cite discoveries made using TGAC data?

We request that any use of data obtained from the TGAC browser be cited in publications using the following format:

  • The Genomic Ascertainment Cohort (TGAC), Bethesda, MD (URL: http://tgac.nhgri.nih.gov) [date (month, year) accessed].

I have identified a rare variant in TGAC that I believe is associated with a specific clinical phenotype. What phenotype data are available for these individuals?

This is the intent and strength of TGAC, as compared to other databases - we very much want to provide these data to you. Some phenotype data are already collected. The data dictionary for ClinSeq participants is located at (link to data dictionary page).

What genome build is the TGAC data based on?

All data are based on GRCh37/hg19.

What version of dbSNP was used to annotate variants?

dbSNP 144.

What version of Gencode was used to annotate variants?

Version 27 (annotated with VEP version 90).

What are the restrictions on data usage?

All data here are released under a Fort Lauderdale Agreement for the benefit of the intramural biomedical community. See the Terms of Use page for details.

How do I report a suspected bug in the TGAC website?

Please email the TGAC Informatics Team

How do I contact the TGAC team if I wish to propose an evaluation of a patient from this dataset?

If you find a variant in this dataset and have a research project that you would like to propose, please complete the project proposal form
