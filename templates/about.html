About TGAC

The Genomic Ascertainment Cohort is a data resource for the study of genetic disease while minimizing the bias of phenotypic ascertainment. An intramural investigator or authorized collaborator can query the TGAC database for a gene or variant, identify that there are one or more individuals within this dataset who harbor the variant, and then propose research projects involving that individual or those individuals. The proposed projects can have a clinical or basic science focus. For example, you might find a heterozygous nonsense variant in the dataset and want to know if that individual has associated nonsense-mediated mRNA decay of the mutant transcript. Similarly, if you have a mouse model in which the heterozygote knockout has a skin condition, you may want to interview or examine patients with a rare loss of function variant in that gene.

If you would like to propose a research project, please complete the research proposal application. The TGAC team will evaluate your proposal and contact you for further discussion. Once a project is accepted, the TGAC team will help re-contact the individuals identified as having variants of interest. Proposals will be prioritized based on scientific interest of the research question as well as feasibility of the request.

Please note that not all research requests will be possible. It is our hope that all individuals in TGAC identified as having variants of interest will want to participate in post hoc projects. However, some participants may decline requests for evaluation. At this time, these data are limited to intramural research program investigators and authorized collaborators.
